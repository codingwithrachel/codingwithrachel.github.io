Hi, I'm Rachel.

If you have somehow stumbled into this place, welcome!

Currently, I'm a developer working in the arts out of New York City.

Before I came to join the tech scene in the Big Apple in the Spring of 2015, I was in working in education in Seoul, Korea, where I was born and raised. Before that, I was completing my undergraduate studies in International Studies at Duke University in Durham, NC. My family also shortly made home in the sunny city of Gainesville, Florida in my childhood years.

New York has been very good to me. I've met amazing people at local meetups, conferences, study groups, and work. What got me into the tech industry and have continued to fascinate me, are the open sharing of code and ideas (you will want to share your hard work with me?!) and the creative problem-solving nature of software development.

These days, when I am not using AngularJS at work, I am learning HTML5 Canvas games, BackboneJS, ReactJS, and d3.js.

As with coding, my love goes out to snowboarding, playing keyboard, watching basketball & soccer, puppies, dark chocolate, and coffee (yes, that's a coffee bean up there in the tab in case you were wondering!).

Projects

Patron Landing Page

Developing the landing Page was my first big project at Patron where I was responsible for the user interface and Heroku deployment. I worked in a small team (designer, backend developer) to collect potential patron & creative lists.

Flappy Bird Clone

As a fun side project, I am creating a clone of the "Flappy Bird" game on the HTML5 canvas. Although the project is still in work in progress, please feel free to play a game and check the code out on Github.

CryptoVeil Chrome Extension

In a team of four, we built a chrome extension on the MEAN stack that helps Gmail users share sensitive data. Users can create groups that have unique keys that will automatically decrypt data for users of that group. You can find it at the Chrome Web Store and on Github.

Microgreens E-commerce Store

In a team of four, we built a single-page, e-commerce application on the MEAN stack where users can create their own blends of microgreens. You can find it here and on Github.
