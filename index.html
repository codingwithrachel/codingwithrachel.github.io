Hi, I'm Rachel.

If you have somehow stumbled into this place, welcome!

Currently, I'm a developer working in fintech out of New York City.

Before I came to join the tech scene in New York in the Spring of 2015, I was in working in education in Seoul, Korea, where I was born and raised. Before that, I was completing my undergraduate studies in International Comparative Studies & Japanese at Duke University in Durham, NC. My family also shortly made home in the sunny city of Gainesville, Florida in my childhood years.

New York has been very good to me. I've met amazing people at local meetups, conferences, study groups, and work. What got me into the tech industry and have continued to fascinate me, are the open sharing of code and ideas (you will want to share your hard work with me?!) and the creative problem-solving nature of software development.

These days, when I am not using Angular2 and NodeJS at work, I am learning ReactJS.

As with coding, my love goes out to snowboarding, playing keyboard, watching basketball & soccer, puppies, dark chocolate, and coffee (yes, that's a coffee bean up there in the tab in case you were wondering!).

Projects

Patron Landing Page

Developing the landing page was my first big AngularJS project at Patron where I was responsible for the user interface and Heroku deployment. I worked in a small team (designer & backend developer) to collect potential patron and creative lists.

Greedy Bird

As a fun side project, I am creating a spin-off of the "Flappy Bird" game on the HTML5 canvas. The project is still in work in progress, but please feel free to play and check the code out on Github.

CryptoVeil Chrome Extension

In a team of four, we built a chrome extension on the MEAN stack that helps Gmail users share sensitive data. Users can create groups that have unique keys that will automatically decrypt data for users of that group. You can find it at the Chrome Web Store and on Github.
